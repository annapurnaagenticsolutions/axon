# The Kingdom of Agents
## *A fable about why we needed a new language*

---

**Format:** 16-page illustrated storybook (8 spreads)
**Dimensions:** 8.5" × 8.5" square (print-friendly) or 1920×1080 (digital spread)
**Art Style:** Warm watercolor + ink line. Think *The Little Prince* meets *The Architecture of Open Source*. Soft parchment textures, hand-lettered captions, architectural ink drawings of the kingdom.
**Color Palette:** Warm amber, deep indigo, sage green, parchment cream, copper accents.

## Art Direction Notes

**Overall Style:**
- Warm, hand-crafted feel. Not digital or corporate.
- Watercolor backgrounds with strong ink line art for characters and architecture.
- Every illustration should feel like it could hang in a children's book or an architecture studio.
- The "code" in the story should look like illuminated manuscript text — beautiful, symbolic, not literal programming.

**Character Design:**
- Agents are humanoid but not strictly human. They have robes that reflect their function (abacus patterns for Calculator, feathered for Writer, star-maps for Search).
- Ada is clearly human — approachable, inventive, warm.
- The cat is a recurring motif — silent observer, comic relief.

**Typography:**
- Story text: Gentle serif (Crimson Text, Merriweather, or Garamond)
- AXON "code" passages: Monospace but styled to look like illuminated manuscript (maybe with a decorative border)
- Headers: Strong serif with subtle flourishes

**Color Progression:**
- Pages 1–4: Muted, brown, parchment-heavy (the problem — bureaucracy)
- Pages 5–6: Brighter, more greens and ambers (the idea)
- Pages 7–10: Warm amber and soft blues (the transformation)
- Pages 11–14: Rich indigo, copper, gold (the power of the language)
- Pages 15–16: Full spectrum warm light (the thriving kingdom)
